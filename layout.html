RDR2 Controls: Arrow Keys + Razer Naga X

Left hand moves. Right thumb does everything else. No finger gymnastics, no panic key-hunting.

Optimized layout for comfort, speed, and clarity

Core idea

The Razer Naga X gives you a 12‑button thumb grid. You use that grid to move almost all actions, weapons, and inventory off the keyboard and onto your right thumb. Your left hand is then free to do one job only: movement and basic interaction on the arrow cluster.

The result: your brain has a simple rule—left hand = move, right thumb = do.

Step 1 – Razer Synapse setup

Naga X side panel
12 thumb buttons → clean, separated bindings

By default, the Naga X side buttons output the top number row: 123…0-=.

  • Recommended: remap them in Razer Synapse to act as Numpad keys instead.
  • This keeps your mouse bindings logically separate from the normal number row.
Synapse glitch fix: if Synapse refuses to apply your new Numpad bindings, restart Synapse or reboot your PC. That usually restores proper mapping.

Step 2 – Left hand: keyboard & arrow keys

Your left hand should only worry about movement, sprinting, jumping, crouching, and basic interaction. No weapon juggling, no inventory hunting.

Step 3 – Right hand: Razer Naga X thumb grid

All the “do something now” actions live under your right thumb. That includes weapon swaps, melee, Dead Eye, reload, horse whistle, and your core menus (satchel, journal, map).

Mouse performance tip

Problem: when you hold your Sprint key (Right Ctrl or Right Shift) and move the mouse to look around, you notice tiny micro‑stutters.

Fix: in the RDR2 control settings, change mouse input from Raw Input to DirectInput. This is a known solution for mouse‑look stuttering on PC.
Summary: left hand = movement & interaction · right thumb = weapons, actions, menus. Adjust any single key, but keep the division of labor the same.
