The S25 Ultra: Field Manual

Hello Mother. Your new phone is less of a telephone and more of a pocket laboratory. Here is how to use the optical sensors properly.

MODULE 1: STERILIZATION & SETUP

Cleaning the Instrument

Before any observation, ensure the lens is clean. The S25 has five lenses on the back. They gather dust easily.

1
Use a soft cloth (or your sleeve, in a pinch) to wipe the glass circles on the back of the phone.
2
Tap the Camera icon. It looks like a simple grey camera.
MODULE 2: MICRO-OBSERVATIONS (MACRO)

Photographing Small Details

Ideal for: Textures, flowers, bread crusts, small insects.

The S25 Ultra has a "Focus Enhancer" which acts like a microscope mode. It activates automatically when you get close.

1
Hold the phone very close to your subject (about 2-3 cm away).
2
Look for two small yellow circles appearing in the bottom left corner of the screen.
[ o o ]
This means the Macro Lens is active.
3
Tap the screen exactly where you want the focus to be (e.g., the petal of the flower).
4
Press the big white button gently to capture the data.
Assignment: Go to the garden or kitchen. Find a piece of sourdough or a leaf. Get as close as possible until the details are sharp. Take the photo.
MODULE 3: LONG RANGE OBSERVATION

Using the Zoom Properly

Do not "pinch" the screen like the old days. It is imprecise. Use the lens preset buttons.

[Visual: A row of numbers on the screen: .6 | 1 | 3 | 5 ]
1
Look at the bottom of the viewfinder for the row of numbers: .6, 1, 3, 5.
2
Tap "3" for a portrait or something across the room.
3
Tap "5" for birds or architectural details on a roof.
!
Avoid anything over "10". The digital noise ruins the data quality.
