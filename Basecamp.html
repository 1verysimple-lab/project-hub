Settlement

A compact Story mode starter camp guide for Green Hell — build strategy, step by step, and what to carry

Overview

This single page explains what to build first in Story mode, why each structure matters, how to assemble a compact starter camp, and what you should carry on missions. The approach favors small, modular bases that are quick to build, easy to resupply, and resilient to scripted events.

Where to place your camp

  • Pick a flat area near water and wood but not inside tribe camps or scripted locations.
  • Prefer riverbanks or small clearings close to resources but keep visibility low to avoid attracting jaguars or NPCs.
  • Keep the base compact so you can replicate it elsewhere if the story forces you to relocate.

How to build step by step

  1. Clear a 6×6 area of bushes and small plants; leave large trees for resources.
  2. Place the shelter first to secure a respawn point.
  3. Build a fire pit and water purifier; boil water before long trips.
  4. Place a storage chest inside or just outside the shelter and move mission items there.
  5. Craft a basic workbench to unlock better tools (machete, spear, bow).
  6. Add a bed and drying rack near the fire for rest and food preservation.
  7. Plant 4–10 crop plots for bamboo if you plan to expand structures later.

Quick placement tips

  • Put the drying rack close to the fire but not directly over it.
  • Keep the chest inside shelter to reduce loss on death.
  • Place workbench a short walk from the shelter so crafting noise doesn’t attract attention.

Risks and trade offs

  • Large bases waste resources and are vulnerable to scripted events.
  • Building too close to tribe areas invites attacks; scout first.
  • Overbuilding early slows progression; expand only when you have steady supplies.

Packing strategy

  • Prioritize water, weapon, and med supplies first; carry only what you will use on the mission.
  • Keep a duplicate small kit in your base chest for quick resupply.
  • Place bandages, water, and a weapon in quick-access slots for fast reaction to bleeding or predators.
